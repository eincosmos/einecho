1 — Hardware & wiring (what you need)

Raspberry Pi 5 (or any Pi with GPIO)

HC-SR04 or waterproof ultrasonic sensor (JSN/HC variant)

Jumper wires

Breadboard (optional)

1 × 1kΩ and 1 × 2kΩ resistors (voltage divider for Echo if needed)

Power: USB power bank / standard Pi power

Wiring (BCM pin numbers example):

TRIG → GPIO 23 (BCM)

ECHO → GPIO 24 (BCM) — use voltage divider if sensor Echo outputs 5V to protect Pi 3.3V

VCC → 5V or 3.3V (depending on sensor)

GND → GND

Quick voltage divider (if echo is 5V):

ECHO → 2kΩ → Pi GPIO
between ECHO and Pi GPIO connect 1kΩ to GND (forms divider: 5V * 1/(1+2) ≈ 1.67V — adjust with proper resistor values e.g. 1k/2k yields ~1.67V, prefer 2k/1k for ~3.3V; follow sensor docs).
